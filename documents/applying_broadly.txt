Q: How many companies should I apply to? I feel like I'm spamming and it's not working.

A (top comment, 912 upvotes):
There's a big difference between applying broadly and applying randomly. Most students who complain that mass applying doesn't work are applying randomly — sending the same generic resume to 200 companies with no thought.

**The actual numbers:**

For a typical student in a normal job market: apply to 50-100 companies across a range of tiers. Expect a 5-15% response rate to get to a screen. Expect a 20-40% success rate from screen to offer. Do the math — you need volume.

For a strong candidate with good experience: 30-50 companies, more targeted.

For a weak market or low experience: 100+ companies, heavy on non-FAANG and non-household names.

**The tier system that works:**

Tier 1 (Reach, ~20% of applications): Places you'd be thrilled to get — FAANG, top quant firms, top unicorns. Apply but don't count on them.

Tier 2 (Target, ~50% of applications): Solid tech companies, growth-stage startups with funding, established mid-size companies in your city. You have a real shot here.

Tier 3 (Safety, ~30% of applications): Local companies, non-tech industries that have dev roles (banking, healthcare, retail all have engineering teams), companies you've never heard of. You should get offers from here if you're doing things right.

A (second comment, 398 upvotes):
Specific places to find opportunities beyond LinkedIn and Indeed:

- **Handshake** for companies specifically targeting students
- **Y Combinator job board** for YC startups (often small teams hungry for help)
- **AngelList/Wellfound** for early-stage startups
- **Your university's job portal** — seriously, employers post here specifically because they want your school's students
- **LinkedIn, but search for "intern" AND filter to "Easy Apply"** — high volume, fast
- **Company career pages directly** — some companies don't post on aggregators

A (third comment, 276 upvotes):
Track everything. Use a spreadsheet with: company name, role, date applied, status, next step, deadline. When you have 80+ applications in flight you will forget where you are with half of them. Missing a deadline or forgetting to follow up on a response is completely avoidable and completely common.

Column headers I use: Company | Role | Applied | Status | Last Contact | Next Step | Notes

A (fourth comment, 201 upvotes):
Don't stop applying after you get a first interview. Keep the pipeline full until you have a signed offer in hand. Interviews fall through. Offers get rescinded. Companies ghost. A pipeline with 3 companies in the interview stage feels safe until all 3 reject you in the same week. Keep applying the whole time.
